is there anything different required for service animal doctor notes and emotional support pet doctor notes

Yes — service animal (SA) documentation and emotional support animal (ESA) letters are different, and the requirements are not the same. 🐕‍🦺📄

1️⃣ Service Animal “Doctor Notes”

For service animals, a traditional doctor’s note usually is not required under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

Instead, businesses can only ask two questions:

Is the dog a service animal required because of a disability?

What work or task has the dog been trained to perform?

Important points:

❌ No official “certificate” or registry required

❌ Businesses usually cannot demand medical documentation

✔ The animal must be trained to perform a task (guide, alert, mobility help, etc.)

However, housing providers sometimes request documentation if the disability is not obvious.

2️⃣ Emotional Support Animal (ESA) Doctor Notes

ESA animals do require a letter from a licensed mental health professional.

Typical ESA letter requirements:

✔ Written by a:

Licensed therapist

Psychologist

Psychiatrist

Physician or other licensed clinician

✔ Must state:

The patient has a mental or emotional disability

The ESA helps alleviate symptoms

Provider license information

✔ Must be on:

Official letterhead

Include date and signature

3️⃣ Key Differences
Feature	Service Animal	Emotional Support Animal
Training required	✅ Yes	❌ No
Doctor note required	❌ Usually not	✅ Yes
Covered by ADA	✅ Yes	❌ No
Housing protection	✅ Yes	✅ Yes (FHA)
Public access	✅ Yes	❌ No
⚠️ Important for a “Doctor’s Note” Service

If you're creating a Doctor’s Note brand or service, you should know:

ESA letters must be written by licensed clinicians

Selling fake certifications or letters can be illegal in many states

Service animal “certificates” are often misleading and unnecessary
